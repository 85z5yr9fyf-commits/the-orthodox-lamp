I. Scripture

01▶

Unless otherwise noted, every Scripture quotation on this site is taken from the Authorized (King James) Version of 1611, which is in the public domain. The KJV was chosen for its public-domain status and its enduring place in the English-speaking Christian tradition.

Throughout the site, Scripture references now carry a dotted underline; hovering (or focusing) a reference displays the full KJV text of the verse, drawn from a copy bundled directly with the site — no external service is contacted.

  • The Holy Bible, Authorized (King James) Version. London, 1611; public domain. Old and New Testaments.

II. Patristic Collections

02▶

The bulk of patristic citation on this site rests on the two great public-domain English collections of the nineteenth century, supplemented by modern critical translations where the older versions are dated or incomplete.

  • The Ante-Nicene Fathers (ANF). Eds. Alexander Roberts & James Donaldson; American edition arranged by A. Cleveland Coxe. 10 vols. Buffalo & New York: Christian Literature Publishing Company, 1885–1887. Public domain. — Writings of the Fathers down to A.D. 325.
  • Nicene and Post-Nicene Fathers, Series 1 (NPNF1). Ed. Philip Schaff. 14 vols. 1886–1890. Public domain. — Augustine and John Chrysostom.
  • Nicene and Post-Nicene Fathers, Series 2 (NPNF2). Eds. Philip Schaff & Henry Wace. 14 vols. 1890–1900. Public domain. — Athanasius, the Cappadocians, Cyril of Jerusalem, Jerome, Ambrose, Ephrem, Eusebius, John of Damascus, and the canons of the Ecumenical Councils.
  • The Fathers of the Church (FOTC). Washington, D.C.: Catholic University of America Press. — Modern critical translations consulted where ANF/NPNF is incomplete, especially for Cyril of Alexandria. (In copyright; cited, not reproduced.)
  • Popular Patristics Series. Crestwood / Yonkers, NY: St Vladimir's Seminary Press. — Modern translations of select works (e.g. Athanasius, On the Incarnation; Cyril of Alexandria, On the Unity of Christ, trans. J. A. McGuckin).

V. Oriental Orthodox Witnesses

05▶

The distinctively non-Chalcedonian and Alexandrian voices central to this site. Much of this material survives in Syriac, Coptic, Greek, and Armenian, and is found in modern critical editions rather than the older English collections.

  • Cyril of Alexandria, On the Unity of Christ, That Christ Is One, the Twelve Anathemas, Commentary on John, festal letters. — FOTC and Popular Patristics editions; some letters in NPNF2, vol. 14 (conciliar acts).
  • Dioscorus of Alexandria, conciliar acts and letters. — Surviving documents in the acts of Ephesus II (449) and Chalcedon (451).
  • Timothy Aelurus, anti-Chalcedonian writings. — Preserved in Syriac; Patrologia Orientalis and critical editions.
  • Severus of Antioch, homilies, letters, and the Philalethes. — Mostly Syriac; Patrologia Orientalis.
  • Shenoute of Atripe, Coptic sermons and treatises. — Critical Coptic editions.

VI. Creeds & Councils

06▶

The Oriental Orthodox Churches receive the first three Ecumenical Councils and reject the Council of Chalcedon (451) as having gone beyond the faith of Cyril; this distinction is treated at length elsewhere on the site.

  • First Council of Nicaea (325) — canons and creed.
  • First Council of Constantinople (381) — the Niceno-Constantinopolitan Creed.
  • Council of Ephesus (431) — affirmation of the Theotokos and condemnation of Nestorius.
  • Second Council of Ephesus (449) — received in the Oriental Orthodox tradition.
  • Council of Chalcedon (451) — not received by the Oriental Orthodox; see the Christology and Chalcedon pages.
  • Conciliar acts and canons in NPNF2, vol. 14 (The Seven Ecumenical Councils, ed. Henry R. Percival).

VII. Modern Editions & Reference Works

07▶

Where the nineteenth-century translations are archaic or where critical scholarship has advanced, modern editions are consulted. These are cited for accuracy but, being in copyright, are never reproduced at length.

  • The Fathers of the Church series (Catholic University of America Press).
  • Popular Patristics Series (St Vladimir's Seminary Press).
  • Patrologia Orientalis and Patrologia Graeca (Migne) for original-language texts.
  • Standard critical studies on the Council of Chalcedon and miaphysite Christology (e.g. the works of J. A. McGuckin and V. C. Samuel) for historical framing.

VIII. A Note on Accuracy

08▶

Quotation accuracy is the first priority of this site. Every patristic quotation is checked against the editions listed above and is marked as either a Direct quotation (verbatim from the cited translation) or a Paraphrase (a faithful summary in our own words). Where a translation differs across editions, the public-domain ANF/NPNF reading is preferred unless a modern critical edition is explicitly noted.

Scripture is quoted from the King James Version throughout. If you find any misattribution or inaccuracy, it is an error to be corrected — the aim is to represent the Fathers and the Scriptures faithfully, never to bend them to a conclusion.
