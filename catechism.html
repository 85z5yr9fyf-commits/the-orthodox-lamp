Start Here — A Path to the Faith

A guided journey from the first question to the church door

Whether you are an inquirer, a Protestant or Catholic exploring the ancient Church, or a skeptic asking whether any of it is even true — this is a path, not a wall of links.

Each step answers one honest question and points you to the pages that go deeper. Walk it in order, or jump to wherever your own doubts live. The lamp is lit; follow it in.

1Is There a God?

▶
“Is there anything beyond the material world?”

Before any question about Jesus, the Church, or the Bible, there is one prior question: is reality the product of blind matter, or of a Mind? Everything downstream depends on the answer, so this is where an honest search begins — not with a leap in the dark, but with reasons.

The Case for GodCause, design, fine-tuning, and contingency — the classic arguments. The Moral ArgumentIf right and wrong are real, where do they come from? The Problem of EvilIf God is good, why is there so much suffering? Science & FaithThe supposed “war” between them is a modern myth.

2Did It Really Happen?

▶
“Did the Resurrection actually happen in history?”

Christianity does not ask for a vague spirituality; it stakes everything on an event — that a crucified man rose from the dead in first-century Judea. That is a historical claim, and it can be examined like one. If Christ is not risen, the Church is a beautiful mistake. If He is, everything changes.

Did Jesus Exist?Even secular historians grant He was a real figure. The ResurrectionThe empty tomb, the witnesses, and the alternatives. Are the Gospels Reliable?How close are they to the events they report? Was Jesus God?Lord, prophet, or legend — the real options.

3Can I Trust the Bible?

▶
“Can I trust the book this all rests on?”

Skeptics say the Bible was edited, mistranslated, and cherry-picked by councils. The Orthodox answer is not to defend a book that fell from heaven, but to show how the Church received, guarded, and canonized the Scriptures — and why the text you hold is trustworthy.

The Canon of ScriptureHow the Church discerned which books are inspired. Has the Bible Been Corrupted?The manuscript evidence, plainly weighed. Hard PassagesAlleged contradictions and difficult texts answered.

4Whose Church?

▶
“Why not just me and my Bible?”

If Christianity is true, Christ did not leave a book and wish us luck — He founded a Church. But there are thousands of churches all reading the same Bible and reaching opposite conclusions. That fact alone tells us that “Scripture alone” cannot settle the question. So where is the Church He actually founded?

“Scripture Alone”?Why the Bible cannot interpret itself in a vacuum. The Authority of the ChurchChrist founded a visible Church that can teach. Apostolic SuccessionThe unbroken line of ordination from the Apostles. So Many DenominationsFinding the one Church amid endless division.

5Why the Orthodox Church?

▶
“Of the ancient churches, which kept the faith?”

The search narrows to the ancient apostolic churches — those that can trace themselves to the Apostles themselves. Here the Orthodox make a bold claim: that they have held “the faith once for all delivered to the saints” unchanged. This step tests that claim against Scripture, the Fathers, and the councils.

How the Church KnowsScripture, Holy Tradition, and the councils together. Why the Church Fathers?The earliest Christians are our living witnesses. The Councils & the SchismWhere the one Church divided, and why it matters. Ancient WorshipA liturgy handed down, not invented.

6Why Oriental Orthodox?

▶
“Why Oriental Orthodox, and not Eastern or Roman?”

Among the Orthodox, one family — the Oriental Orthodox — was cut off in 451 over a single word, accused of a heresy they never held. Their Christology is not “Monophysite” but Miaphysite: one incarnate nature of God the Word, fully God and fully man. This step explains that confession and the communion that keeps it.

Miaphysite ChristologyOne incarnate nature — why it is not “Monophysite.” Miaphysite vs DyophysiteThe two languages of Christology, side by side. Eastern & Oriental OrthodoxHow the two Orthodox families relate today. The Oriental Orthodox FamilyCoptic, Armenian, Ethiopian, Syriac & Indian.

▶
“I’m convinced — now what?”

Conviction becomes life not through more arguments but through the door of a parish. The Orthodox faith is entered on your knees, in worship, fasting, and the Mysteries — not merely in the mind. Here are the practical next steps.

A Word Before You Go

No website makes anyone Orthodox. Reading ends; the Liturgy begins. If this path has stirred something in you, the next step is not another article — it is to find a parish, speak with a priest, and come and see.

“Come and see.” It is how the first disciples were called, and it is still the only real answer.
